Matterhorn North Face Sunset

Hand printed Serigraph – Pencil on paper, black & white sketch 70x53 cm

Available as a Limited Edition of 500. Artist signed, hand printed Serigraphs on museum quality handmade Zerkall bütton paper.

The classic Zermatt view of the sun setting on the Matterhorn's north face, as seen in the spring, when there is often a combination of summer sun and winter snow. This brings out the features of the Horn's north face in the late evening and is one of most stunning times of the year to look up at this amazing pyramid of rock.

Serigraph Prints.

This is a Serigraph print of the original artwork. Great efforts to source paper of a quality suitable for this process. A German "bütton" (Mould-made) paper from the traditions-rich Zerkall paper mill has been selected to meet the highest possible standards.

The Matterhorn Sunset on the North Wall Edition is printed on a 270g Zerkall Litho VI Bütton paper. This "mould-made" paper is one of the best options for creating this type of Serigraph. It is 75% pure cotton, acid free, and does not use optical brighteners. It is museum grade — which means that is non-ageing and does not yellow over time (meeting the ISO 9706 standard).

Framing.

At the moment we do not offer any framing service. It is something we are looking intro offering a bit further down the line. Obviously, getting your print framed properly is an important part of displaying it to its full potential. As soon as we find companies that offer a quality product/service, we will provide the links to them here (European, North American and Asian online framing companies).

The prints are made slightly oversized, to give you the most options in how to frame and display your print – allowing you to best match the space your have available.

The simplest option is to place the print in a frame without a Passpartout (English: Matted). Trim your print to the size of your frame.

Single or double Passpartout/Matts really bring the print to life. Another option is to cover the print with an appropriate plexiglass or real glass cover. The prints are of the highest quality (paper & ink), but if you're planning on hanging it in direct sunlight or are looking to protect it from things like dust, dirt, bugs, then you might want to look into some sort of UV protection with the plexiglass or real glass.
